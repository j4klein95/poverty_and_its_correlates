Poverty in the US counties

The US Census Data collects data for more than 3000+ counties through more than 34 variables, such as employment, transportation description, type of employment.


I. Preliminary Findings

Before modeling Poverty, some potential strong correlations were identified.

General Overview

Wyoming, Vermont, North Dakota and Alaska are the States with the majority of counties in poverty


More Info

Exploring Poverty

The preliminary exploration lead to identify strong correlation between poverty and ethnicity, income, %pop who finished high school, and unemployment

Data Tool: Correlation Heatmap


II. Classification of counties

Based on the preliminary findings, the second step is to identfify and cnsecuently clasified the more important variable that influence Poverty in the counties

High School Rates correlates to Poverty levels

A "unit" increase in the unemployment rate is associated with a 0.188130 "unit" increase in the percentage of population who does not finish college. R~0.912

Method Applied: OLS Regression over 10 variables. More Info

Having a car decisive to Poverty

Decision tree Classification indicate that Having a Car is the most important feature of the model with the highest Gini value of 0.5


Method Applied: Decision Tree. More Info

III. Excluding Income and Unemployment

After replicating the decision tree model, over different escenarios income per capita, and unemployment were the most important features in the model. To identify other relationships they were excluded of the analysis.

Race an important feature

Counties with population native american or black are more likely to being classified as in Poverty (feature perfomance Gini 0.70)

Method Applied: Decision Tree. More Info
